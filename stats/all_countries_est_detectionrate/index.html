Estimated Detection Rate By Country

The following table contains a ranking of countries based on the estimated detection rate of confirmed cases. The model used behind is based on on the approach Vollmer and Bommer, University of Goettingen proposed.

Note that it only provides an estimation of the detection rate as of fourteen days ago (2021-05-25).

Not as well, that the approach used here is usable, if the country’s people have been exposed to the virus for several weeks: Reason for that is that the number of reported deaths is used as basis for the model. The latter is usually under-reported by officials at the beginning of an epidemy.

Estimated detection rates significantly above 100% provide indication that the number of deaths reported by the country is not reported properly.

Estimated detection rates are not static: They are influenced by many factors and can also vary over time, especially if countries apply measures against infections (or if they do properly report all deaths related to the disease).
